Kat McNeely-White

Colorado State University · katherinelmwhite@gmail.com

I am a Ph.D. student in the Cognitive Psychology program at Colorado State University studying under Dr. Anne Cleary.

​My research focuses on recognition memory processes in humans, specifically during instances of retrieval failure. Frequently, people fail to remember the exact details of how they met someone, where they left something, or when they were supposed to meet someone. During these moments, although the details are momentarily inaccessible, people still have access to other information about the memory. My research examines how people are able to make decisions during retrieval failure by relying on familiarity signals. To study this phenomenon, known as recognition without identification, we use a variety of methodologies, including behavioral paradigms and functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI).
